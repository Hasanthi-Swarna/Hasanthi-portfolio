Local Services Discovery Platform

Role: Product Management (First PM Case Study)

Context: Self-initiated

Product Thinking Marketplace User Research MVP

Problem

In large gated communities located far from city centers (housing 1000–1500 families), residents struggle to access nearby services such as food delivery, salons, and home services through existing apps. Most mainstream platforms either do not serve these locations or are not optimized for hyperlocal needs.

At the same time, many residents within these communities run cloud kitchens, home salons, and small service businesses. However, service discovery depends largely on word-of-mouth or WhatsApp groups, which are fragmented, unreliable, and limited by social reach.

As a result, residents remain unaware of available local services, while service providers struggle to reach consistent customers—despite demand and supply existing within the same community.

Why This Problem Matters

Living in a gated community highlighted how local services remain underutilized despite being readily available. The absence of a structured discovery platform results in poor utilization of local talent, increased inconvenience for residents, and missed opportunities for service providers.

Users

  • Residents: Need fast, reliable, and nearby services for daily needs.
  • Service Providers: Need consistent discovery and a simple way to reach customers within the community.

User Personas

  • Anamika (35): Needs fresh food after returning from work.
  • Rahul (24): Wants affordable, healthy daily lunch options.
  • Swetha (32): Runs a cloud kitchen but lacks marketing reach.
  • Rena (36): A retired salon professional offering home services.

Solution

A community-first service discovery platform that connects residents with local service providers within the same gated community. The platform enables service providers to list offerings, availability, and pricing, while residents can browse, order services, or book appointments.

This centralized approach reduces friction, removes dependency on informal promotion channels, and enables trusted, hyperlocal service exchange.

Key Features (MVP)

  • Service listings restricted to the user’s gated community
  • Order placement and appointment booking
  • Vendor profiles with menus or service details

Success Metrics

  • Service discovery rate
  • Repeat orders or bookings
  • Active service providers per community

Learnings

This case study helped me understand how real-world problems require balancing user needs, feasibility, and prioritization. It reinforced the importance of defining clear problems, validating assumptions, and designing simple MVPs before scaling solutions.
